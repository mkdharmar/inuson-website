Market Context

The engineering talent shortage is structural, not cyclical

The United States faces a sustained shortage of qualified engineers across nearly every discipline. The causes are structural: a large cohort of experienced engineers is retiring, new graduates lack the applied project experience that complex roles require, and demand driven by infrastructure investment, manufacturing reshoring, and technology expansion continues to outpace supply.

For organizations competing for mechanical, electrical, and civil engineers, the search cannot rely on job postings. The best candidates — those with proven project experience and the judgment to work independently — are not actively looking. They are employed, performing well, and accessible only through direct outreach from a recruiter who knows the field and has earned the right to have a credible conversation.
